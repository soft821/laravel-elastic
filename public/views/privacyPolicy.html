alifeofriley.com Privacy Policy

We ask that you read this privacy policy of the website www.alifeofriley.com (the “Website”) carefully as it contains important information on who we are, how and why we collect, store, use and share personal data, your rights in relation to your personal data and on how to contact us and supervisory authorities in the event you have a complaint.

Who we are: Life of Riley OÜ, a company organised under the laws of Estonia, with the Company Code 14412858; owner (or beneficiary, as applicable) of all intellectual property associated with the Website and the services available on the same (“We”, “we”, “our”, “us”, or “Life of Riley”, as applicable).

Life of Riley collects, uses and is responsible for certain personal data, as the term is understood under the General Data Protection Regulation (the “GDPR”), about you. When we do so we are regulated under the GDPR which applies across the European Union (including in the United Kingdom) and we are responsible as ‘controller’ of that personal data for the purposes of those laws.

Information collected by us from you

  1. To enable the execution, performance and enforcement of the terms and conditions of the Website, which enable the provision of the services available on the Website (“Purpose A”) we collect the following personal data when you provide it to us:
    • Full name; and
    • Email address.
  2. To send you promotional and marketing communications about Life of Riley products and services (“Purpose B”) we collect the following personal data when you provide it to us:
    • Full name; and
    • Email address.

Whether information has to be provided by you, and if so why The provision of your name and email address in support of Purpose A is required to enable Purpose A and to use the services available on the Website. The provision of your name and email address in support of purpose B is not required to use the services available on the Website.

How long your personal data will be kept

  • We will hold your name and email address obtained in support of Purpose A, for as long as necessary to enable Purpose A, and therefore as long as you are a user of the Website.
  • We will hold your name and email address obtained in support of Purpose B, until the earlier to occur or your withdrawal of your consent for Purpose B, or our decision to stop sending you marketing and promotional communications about our activities.

Reasons we can collect and use your personal data Our processing of your name and email address in support of Purpose A is necessary to enable Purpose A, in support of the terms and conditions of the Website.

We rely on your consent to process your name and email address in support of Purpose B, as the lawful basis on which we collect and use your name and email address.

Your rights Under the General Data Protection Regulation, you have a number of important rights free of charge. In summary, those include rights to:

  • fair processing of information and transparency over how we use your use personal data;
  • access to your personal data and to certain other supplementary information that this privacy policy is already designed to address;
  • require us to correct any mistakes in your information which we hold;
  • require the erasure of personal data concerning you in certain situations;
  • receive the personal data concerning you which you have provided to us, in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format and have the right to transmit those data to a third party in certain situations;
  • object at any time to the processing of personal data concerning you for direct marketing;
  • object to decisions being taken by automated means which produce legal effects concerning you or similarly significantly affect you;
  • object in certain other situations to our continued processing of your personal data;
  • otherwise restrict our processing of your personal data in certain circumstances; and
  • claim compensation for damages caused by our breach of any data protection laws.

If you would like to exercise any of those rights, please:

  • email info@livethelifeofriley.com if you are a provider of events; and info@alifeofriley.com for all other users of the Website.
  • let us have enough information to identify you (e.g. account number, user name, registration details);
  • let us have proof of your identity and address (a copy of your driving licence or passport and a recent utility or credit card bill); and
  • let us know the information to which your request relates, including any account or reference numbers, if you have them.

If you would like to unsubscribe from any email newsletter you can also click on the ‘unsubscribe’ button at the bottom of the email newsletter. It may take up to 1 week for this to take place.

Keeping your personal data secure We have appropriate security measures in place to prevent personal data from being accidentally lost, or used or accessed in an unauthorised way. We limit access to your personal data to those who have a genuine business need to know it. Those processing your information will do so only in an authorised manner and are subject to a duty of confidentiality.

We also have procedures in place to deal with any suspected data security breach. We will notify you and any applicable regulator of a suspected data security breach where we are legally required to do so.

Who we share your personal data with We share only your name (not email or social media information) with event organisers whom you ask us to send your information to via the Website, in support of Purpose A. Some of those third party recipients may be based outside the European Economic Area.

We routinely share your information with The Progression Agency, a company organised under the rules of the United Arab Emirates (the “UAE”), resident at, Media City, Dubai (the “PA”), because the PA part-manages the Website on our behalf.

Until the European Commission has given a formal decision that the UAE provides an adequate level of data protection similar to those which apply in the EEA, any transfer of your personal data will be subject to a data processing agreement between PA and Life of Riley (the “Data Processing Agreement”) as permitted under the GDPR, which isTo obtain a copy of the such safeguards; or if you would like further information please contact info@livethelifeofriley.com if you are a provider of events; and Info@alifeofriley.com for all other users of the Website.

We will not otherwise transfer your personal data outside of the EEA or to any organisation (or subordinate bodies) governed by public international law or which is set up under any agreement between two or more countries.

We will share personal data with law enforcement or other authorities if required by applicable law.

We will not share your personal data with any other third party.

How to complain We hope that we can resolve any query or concern you raise about our use of your information.

The GDPR also gives you the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority, in particular in the European Union (or European Economic Area) state where you work, normally live or where any alleged infringement of data protection laws occurred.
